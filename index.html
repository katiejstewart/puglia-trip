Before you go

Know before you go

Early August timing

These dates land just before Ferragosto (Aug 15), so you skip the single busiest day — but it's still peak season with highs in the 90s°F and full towns.

  • Sightsee before ~11 AM; reserve afternoons for pool and spa.
  • Book hotels, the spa, and dinners well ahead — they sell out this week.
  • Confirm restaurant hours; several close one weekday in summer.

Pacing for two

The towns are charming because they're old — cobblestones, steps, hills. All are doable slowly.

  • Your two resorts are the flat, restful anchors between outings.
  • Isola Bella's beach and Castelmola have real stairs — both have overlooks you can enjoy without the climb.
  • Linen, a hat, sun protection, and comfortable closed walking shoes.

Booking notes

A few things worth locking early:

  • Rome is the gateway — nonstop both ways from Miami (American & ITA), with short hops to Bari and Catania.
  • For the Aug 7 return, book Catania → Rome → Miami on one ITA ticket so the connection is protected.
  • Private transfers: airport→masseria, and Catania→Taormina.
  • Spa treatments at both resorts at the moment you check in.

Good to know

  • Italy uses Type F/L plugs, 230V — bring adapters.
  • Carry some cash; smaller town spots can be card-shy.
  • Driving is optional — with private transfers and walkable bases you don't need a rental car.
  • Times and fares above are indicative; confirm exact schedules at booking.
